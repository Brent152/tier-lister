User does not like exfoliating soaps. When recommending soaps (e.g. from Bulk Apothecary's cold process line), exclude anything labeled "Scrub" or anything that lists sea salt, oatmeal, silica sand, pumice, ground seeds/shells, peppermint leaves, etc. as exfoliants in the bar.

**Why:** Stated explicitly after I recommended several scrubs. Note: their existing tier list (Soaps-preuse) still ranks some scrubs (Tobacco Flowers Scrub at S, Kashmir Scrub at B, Mango Salsa Scrub at B) — those were rated "pre-use" on scent description alone and don't override the stated preference for new purchases.

**How to apply:** When filtering Bulk Apothecary's catalog for recommendations, drop anything with "Scrub" in the product name. Also be cautious of bars that mention oatmeal/sand/salt as additives in the description (e.g. Vanilla Oatmeal, Oatmeal Spice, Pine Tar — even when not labeled "Scrub"). Clay/mud bars (e.g. Nag Champa Mud) are likely fine since clay isn't gritty.

Taste profile from the tier list (separate from the scrub filter):
- Loves: warm/sweet/complex with depth — musk, amber, vanilla, patchouli, sandalwood, baking spice, smoke/charcoal
- Likes: warm-spice fruit, citrus with earthy/woody depth
- Cool on: clean/herbal/mint/green, generic lily florals
- Hates: heavy traditional floral bouquets (English Garden was F-tier)
